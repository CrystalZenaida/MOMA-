The Tower of Fla-Pisa
Parody of the Tower of Pisa and the “Flappy Bird” meme.
Flappy Bird is a mobile game created by Vietnamese developer Dong Nguyen that gained a speedy notoriety for its challenging and addictive nature; eventually becoming the top downloaded app on both Google Play and Apple App Store on January 31th.

The Persistence of Persistence
Parody of Salvador Dali’s The Persistence of Memory and Filthy Frank’s “It’s Time to Stop” meme.
"It’s Time to Stop” is notable green screen video of many by YouTube personality Filthy Frank. The video, which shows Filthy Frank walking into frame with an oversized clock, shaking it and shouting the phrase, has been edited and used across numerous social media outlets.

The Sponge
Parody of Edvard Munch’s The Scream and the “Primitive Spongebob” meme.
Primitive Spongebob is a character from Nickelodeon’s Spongebob Squarepants who was featured in the episode “SB-129.” A still from this episode, which shows the character in an alert/surprised expression, had become popular across social media outlets as a reaction image macro used to visually express situations of varying surprise and startling.

A Cup of Tea with Kermit
Parody of Mary Cassatt’s A Cup of Tea and the “But that’s None of my Business” meme.
“But That’s None of My Business” is an image macro series that features the character, Kermit the Frog, from The Muppets drinking a cup of Lipton Tea while calling out an individual or group and using “But That’s None of My Business” as a sarcastic postscript.

Spatial Concept 'Loss'
Parody of Lucio Fontana’s Spatial Concept ‘Waiting’ and the Loss.jpg meme.
The Loss.jpg meme is a series of parodies based on an unusually dramatic and poorly-executed comic from the webcomic series Ctrl+Alt+Del which became the subject of mockery across social media. One popular form of parody is to take the comic strip and reinterpret it in minimalistic ways which range from lines and dots to emojis to different Intellectual Properties.

The Conspiratorial Mirror
Parody of René Magritte’s The False Mirror and the Illuminati meme.
The Illuminati is a purported secret society of world governments and public figures with the ultimate goal of establishing a global totalitarian government. Its long history in American Mythology has made it prevalent in many online conspiracy theories and the subject of mockery by many who are skeptical of its existence. A symbol often associated with the organization is the “Eye of Providence,” an image of an eye enclosed within a triangle commonly found on the back of the US one-dollar bill.

Harambe’s Creation of Adam
Parody of Michelangelo’s The Creation of Adam and the Harambe meme.
Harambe is a Western Lowland Silverback gorilla that was killed in May 2016 at the Cincinnati Zoo by employees in order to save a child that fell into the gorilla’s enclosure. After his death, a public controversy followed as to whether Harambe should have been tranquilized rather than shot dead, and as to whether place blame on the child’s parents for Harambe’s death. This controversy ultimately led to many strange tributes to Harambe including the infamous “Dicks Out for Harambe.”

Self Portrait? Not Sure…
Parody of a Vincent van Gogh Self Portrait and the “Not Sure If” meme.
The “Not Sure If…” meme is an image macro series that features a still of the character, Fry, from the adult cartoon Futurama. In the still illustrates Fry squinting and is usually accompanied by text used to represent an internal monologue.

Water Lilies and Magikarp
Parody of Claude Monet’s Water Lilies series and the Magikarp meme.
b. Magikarp is a creature that resembles a Koi Fish from the game series Pokémon. Magikarp is often ridiculed players for essentially being useless until it evolves into its second form named Gyarados, a powerful Sea-Dragon-like creature.

Nyan Cat Pop-tARt
Parody of Pop Art and the “Nyan Cat” meme.
Nyan Cat is an 8-bit animation created by illustrator Chris Torres depicting a grey cat with the body of a pop tart flying through outer space, leaving a rainbow trail behind it. The surreal humor of the piece had garnered mass popularity across social media and art communities, inspiring fan illustrations and other forms of media.

Tag Yourself, Campbell’s Soup
Parody of Andy Warhol’s Campbell’s Soup Series and the “Tag Yourself” meme.
Tag Yourself is a survey meme in which the participant is shown a lineup of characters and are prompted to identify one that they can relate to the most by reblogging the original post and tagging it with their chosen character’s name.

Still Life with Pug Loaf
Parody of Julian Merrow-Smith’s Still Life with Autumn Fruits and the Pug Loaf Meme.
Pug Loaf is an image of Pug that strongly resembles a loaf of bread. It is used frequently in image macros.

No 2 (Blue, Brown, and Green)
Parody of Mark Rothko’s No 2 (Blue, Red, Green) and the “Rare Pepe” meme.
“Pepe the Frog” is a character from Matt Furie’s Boy’s Club and has been used across social media as a reaction images for various expressions including the phrase “Feels good man.” The “Rare Pepe” is a sub-meme of the character in which social media users created and shared different and “rare” variations of Pepe as if the images were trading cards.

The Dog of Man
Parody of René Magritte’s The Son of Man and Joe Gran’s “Dog of Wisdom” meme.
“Dog of Wisdom” is a CGI video created by Joe Gran about a dog on a plane encountering a dog on a cloud who calls himself the Dog of Wisdom and hold a conversation almost entirely in gibberish if not for the subtitles. The video has gained more than 8 million views on YouTube and has spawned many parodies across social media.

Dat Golden Boi
Parody of Andy Warhol’s Gold Marilyn Monroe and the “Dat Boi” meme.
“Dat Boi” is a phrase associated with a 3D model of a green frog on a unicycle, accompanied by the captions, “here comes dat boi!” followed by “o shit waddup!” Various iterations of Dat Boi have been spread across social media outlets.

Doges Playing Poker
Parody of Cassius Marcellus Coolidge’s A Friend in Need, and the Doge and Pun Husky Memes.
b. Doge is a slang term for “dog” and is often associated with the Shiba Inu dog breed, but more popularly associated with a Shiba Inu named Kabosu owned by a Japanese kindergarten teacher named Atsuko Sato. Many images were taken of Kabosu and edited by social media users to include simple broken speech made to resemble the dog’s thoughts. Pun Husky is a series of photographs of a Husky playing with a stuffed toy that was uploaded by reddit user, lawlissmodia. Many saw the Husky’s series of expressions as a set up and punch line of a joke, and thus many users edited in actual jokes to fit in with the image series.

Olympic Viking
Parody of an Untitled and Unclaimed Olympic Rowing Photograph and the Laundry Room Viking Meme.
The Laundry Room Viking is an image macro series that uses a facebook photo of a man using a broom and a plastic waste bin to tread his way through his flooded laundry room after Hurricane Irene in August 2011. Many parodies of this image were created with different captions that echoed the image’s original caption of “do the laundry they said, it will be fun they said.”

Untitled Film Still with Shia LaBeouf #23
Parody of Cindy Sherman’s Untitled Film Still #23 and Shia LaBeouf’s Motivational Speech Meme.
Shia LaBeouf’s Motivational Speech is a green screen video of the actor shouting inspirational messages to a camera while making bizarre gestures. The video itself has inspired numerous parodies and has garnered more than 20 million views.
